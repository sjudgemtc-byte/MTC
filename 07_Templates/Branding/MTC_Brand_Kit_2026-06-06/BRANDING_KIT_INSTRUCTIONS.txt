MTC Branding Kit Instructions

Saved: 2026-06-06

Purpose: practical guidance for building MTC teaching collateral, slide decks, handouts, and simple branded assets using the files saved in this brand kit.

Important note:
- This guide is based on the public MTC FutureReady website, the saved Living Our Brand Guidebook PDF, and the downloaded logo and icon files in this folder.
- It should be treated as a working collateral guide, not a replacement for any internal master brand manual.

Source material reviewed

- Website: https://www.mtcfutureready.org.au/
- Brand guide PDF: Brand_Guidelines/MTC FutureReady_LivingOurBrand_July2025.pdf
- Logos: RGB and CMYK logo files in this folder
- Icons: Icons/

Brand essence

Use collateral that feels:
- optimistic
- practical
- encouraging
- human
- future-focused
- clear rather than corporate-heavy

Key brand language drawn from the guidebook:
- Our purpose is helping you find yours.
- Start here. Think big.
- The further we look, the further we'll go.
- We're with you all the way.
- We'll be your biggest cheerleader.

Core values to reflect in teaching collateral:
- Empathy
- Achievement
- Innovation
- Collaboration

Visual direction

The overall MTC look is:
- bold and high-contrast
- simple and modern
- black, white, and bright yellow as the main visual anchors
- supported by flat illustration and iconography
- generous with whitespace
- friendly, not overly formal

For classroom materials, prefer:
- strong headings
- short supportive copy
- one key message per section
- large icons or illustrations rather than dense decoration

Colour guidance

Primary colours observed across the site and collateral:
- MTC yellow: #FEF153
- Black: #000000
- White: #FFFFFF

Secondary/support colours seen lightly on the public site:
- Dark charcoal: #3F474F
- Accent blue: #2EA3F2

Recommended collateral use:
- Use #FEF153 as the hero background, highlight block, callout strip, or accent panel.
- Use black text on yellow or white for strongest readability.
- Use white logo or white text only on dark backgrounds.
- Keep blue and other secondary colours to a minimum unless the source material clearly calls for them.

Typography

Public web styling strongly suggests:
- Rethink Sans as the preferred modern brand face

Practical use:
- If Rethink Sans is available, use it for headings and subheadings.
- If it is not available in PowerPoint or Word, use a clean sans-serif fallback such as Arial, Aptos, or Calibri.
- Keep headings bold and large.
- Keep body copy straightforward and readable.

Typography style:
- headlines should be bold, simple, and confident
- body text should be short, conversational, and uncluttered
- avoid decorative fonts
- avoid compressed text blocks

Logo use

Main logo files:
- MTC_Logo_RGB.png
- MTC_Logo_Black_RGB.png
- MTC_Logo_White_RGB.png
- MTC_Logo_Rev_RGB.png
- CMYK equivalents for print

Recommended use:
- Use RGB files for PowerPoint, Word, online handouts, and screen use.
- Use CMYK files only for print workflows.
- Use the standard colour logo by default.
- Use the black logo on very light backgrounds where the standard logo feels too busy.
- Use the white logo on dark photographic or dark colour backgrounds.

Logo placement:
- top left or bottom left works well for teaching materials
- keep plenty of clear space around the logo
- do not stretch, recolour, or add effects
- do not crowd the logo with other graphics

Icon use

Icon library:
- see Icons/

The icon style is:
- simple
- bold
- friendly
- easy to read at a glance
- suitable for services, steps, themes, and activity categories

Use icons:
- one icon per idea or topic block
- at a generous size so they remain legible
- consistently across a deck or worksheet
- near headings, section dividers, process steps, or program summaries

Avoid:
- mixing too many icon styles in one piece
- using several small icons where one larger icon would be clearer
- placing icons so close to text that the layout feels cramped

Imagery and illustration

From the website and guidebook, the supporting style leans toward:
- people-first imagery
- real participants and practical outcomes
- optimistic, supportive scenes
- clean illustration with strong outlines

For teaching collateral:
- choose images that show participation, progress, confidence, and support
- prefer warm, genuine photos over glossy stock imagery
- if using illustration, keep it simple and high-contrast

Tone of voice

Write in a way that is:
- encouraging
- direct
- practical
- calm
- positive
- inclusive

Good writing cues:
- help people feel capable
- focus on progress and possibility
- speak with warmth, not hype
- be clear about the next step

Useful phrases already aligned to the brand:
- Start here. Think big.
- Ready to create your future?
- We're with you all the way.
- Helping you take practical steps towards the future you want.
- With us by your side, you're never alone.

Avoid writing that feels:
- cold
- overly bureaucratic
- jargon-heavy
- pushy
- negative or deficit-framed

PowerPoint and teaching collateral rules

For slide decks:
- Use one bold title and one supporting message per slide.
- Use yellow feature slides to break up sections.
- Use icons to introduce modules, activities, or learning themes.
- Keep text short and readable from the back of a room.
- Use black text on white or yellow backgrounds as the default.

Suggested slide structure:
- title slide with strong yellow field and logo
- section divider slides using one icon and a short phrase
- content slides with simple headings, short bullets, and clear spacing
- closing slide with logo and an encouraging call to action

For handouts and worksheets:
- keep margins open
- use strong section headers
- use icons to label exercises or categories
- avoid overfilling pages with text

Simple do and don't guide

Do:
- use the saved MTC logos and icons from this kit
- keep layouts bold and uncluttered
- lean on yellow, black, and white
- write in a supportive and forward-looking tone
- keep the content human and practical

Don't:
- invent a different colour system unless a specific program requires it
- use overly formal corporate templates
- crowd slides with text
- stretch or restyle the logo
- mix unrelated illustration styles

When building new MTC collateral, default to:
1. yellow, black, and white
2. bold sans-serif headings
3. simple icon-led sections
