# Classroom Office One-Month Implementation Plan

Prepared: 2026-06-07

Purpose: introduce an office-style classroom model over 4 weeks in a way that is realistic, calm, and easy to sustain with 15 students.

Use with:
- `2026-06-07_MTC_Redfern_RED-01_Pathway_Classroom-Office_Roles-and-Responsibilities.md`

## Main goal

By the end of 1 month, the class should be able to:
- arrive and follow a routine
- sign in and sign out of computers
- work within rotating role teams
- complete simple admin and support tasks
- help the classroom run more like a workplace

## Important principle

Do not launch everything at once.

The first month should focus on:
- routine first
- clarity first
- repetition first
- success first

Only add new parts when the previous part is working.

## Month overview

Week 1:
- introduce the idea
- set up the daily routine
- test the computer sign-in system
- trial only 2 or 3 simple classroom roles

Week 2:
- expand to all 5 role areas
- assign small responsibilities
- begin role rotation

Week 3:
- strengthen independence
- add team leads
- use daily checklists and simple record-keeping

Week 4:
- run the room more consistently like a small office
- review what is working
- simplify or adjust what is not working

## Week 1: Start small and build routine

### Main objective

Create a predictable start and end to each class.

### What to introduce

- one computer sign-in / sign-out system
- one daily classroom routine
- one short explanation of the office-style model
- 2 or 3 simple role areas only

### Suggested Week 1 roles

- Reception
- IT Support
- Resources and Printing

### What students need to learn this week

- how to arrive and sign in
- where to sit
- how to find the correct computer or task
- how to pack up and sign out
- that roles are part of class, not an optional extra

### Teacher actions for Week 1

- explain the classroom office idea in simple language
- choose the first role teams yourself
- keep tasks very small and visible
- model exactly what each role looks like
- praise correct routine more than speed

### Week 1 daily routine example

At the start:
- students arrive
- students sign into computers
- Reception checks who is present
- IT Support helps with access issues
- Resources team prepares handouts

At the end:
- students sign out
- resources are collected
- room is checked

### Week 1 success signs

- most students sign in correctly
- students understand the idea of having a role
- the room starts more smoothly
- there is less confusion about setup

## Week 2: Add the full role structure

### Main objective

Move from routine only to structured team responsibility.

### What to introduce

- all 5 role areas
- 5 groups of 3 students
- a simple visible role roster
- short role descriptions

### Role areas for Week 2

- Reception
- IT Support
- Administration
- Resources and Printing
- Operations and Records

### What students need to learn this week

- each group has a specific responsibility
- different students will do different jobs
- office-style work requires teamwork and follow-through
- classroom systems depend on everyone doing their part

### Teacher actions for Week 2

- assign roles clearly
- read through each role with the class
- keep responsibilities practical and short
- check each team during the session
- stop and reset if instructions become unclear

### Suggested Week 2 additions

- Reception checks sign-ins
- Administration checks whether the form entries look complete
- Operations checks room reset at the end

### Week 2 success signs

- students can explain their role
- each group completes at least one useful support task
- fewer small classroom jobs fall back on the teacher
- students begin to act more like a team

## Week 3: Build independence and consistency

### Main objective

Shift from teacher-directed roles to more independent role performance.

### What to introduce

- team leads
- simple team checklists
- more student responsibility for follow-through
- short verbal reporting back to the trainer

### What students need to learn this week

- how to complete a task without waiting for constant prompting
- how to report a problem clearly
- how to support classmates professionally
- how to finish the role, not just start it

### Teacher actions for Week 3

- appoint a lead in each team
- teach the lead to check, not boss
- ask each team for a quick update during or after class
- begin stepping back from small routine tasks

### Suggested Week 3 improvements

- IT Support checks all computers are ready before the session begins
- Administration helps with file naming or simple spreadsheet checks
- Operations completes a simple end-of-day checklist

### Week 3 success signs

- teams need less prompting
- students remind each other about routines
- team leads can report what was done
- sign-in and sign-out becomes normal classroom behaviour

## Week 4: Stabilise the office model

### Main objective

Make the model feel normal, useful, and sustainable.

### What to introduce

- regular rotation of roles
- a short review of what each team is doing well
- small adjustments to remove anything that is too hard or not useful

### What students need to learn this week

- roles rotate, but standards stay the same
- every role matters
- office-style work means reliability and cooperation
- systems improve over time

### Teacher actions for Week 4

- rotate groups into new roles
- keep the role descriptions visible
- ask students what is helping and what is confusing
- remove any task that creates more work than value
- keep only the routines that genuinely help

### Week 4 success signs

- the room starts and ends in a more organised way
- students understand the office model
- systems feel repeatable
- students take more responsibility for the shared space

## Suggested weekly focus summary

Week 1:
- routine
- sign-in system
- simple visible support jobs

Week 2:
- full role structure
- role understanding
- shared responsibility

Week 3:
- independence
- team leads
- checklists

Week 4:
- consistency
- rotation
- review and improve

## What to prepare before starting

Before Week 1, prepare:
- a computer sign-in / sign-out form
- a role roster
- a short explanation for students
- a visible daily task board

Before Week 2, prepare:
- role cards or a role sheet
- 5 student groups
- short role expectations

Before Week 3, prepare:
- simple checklists for each team
- team lead names

Before Week 4, prepare:
- the next rotation list
- a quick review discussion prompt

## What not to do in the first month

Do not:
- overload students with too many tasks
- let roles become punishment
- create complicated paperwork
- ask students to manage sensitive records
- assume students understand workplace behaviour without modelling it
- keep adding systems if the first ones are not working yet

## Best first systems to lock in

If you only get 4 things working well in the first month, make them:
- computer sign in / sign out
- role roster
- daily start routine
- end-of-day reset

These will give the biggest return for the least complexity.

## End-of-month review questions

At the end of the month, ask:
- Are students signing in and out consistently?
- Which roles are helping the classroom most?
- Which tasks are too hard, unclear, or unnecessary?
- Which students are ready for more responsibility?
- What should stay exactly the same next month?
- What should be simplified?

## Recommended next step after this month

After the first month, create:
- role cards
- a weekly rotation roster
- a daily checklist sheet
- one or two student-led admin systems such as forms or trackers
