# Classroom Office Roles and Responsibilities

Prepared: 2026-06-07

Purpose: create a simple office-style classroom environment for 15 students that supports workplace habits, shared responsibility, and practical learning.

## Overall model

The classroom runs like a small office.

Students are expected to:
- arrive and sign in
- take responsibility for a role
- complete routine admin tasks
- communicate clearly
- support shared systems
- sign out and help reset the room

This model is designed to:
- help classroom management
- reduce pressure on the trainer
- build workplace behaviour
- teach digital and admin skills
- make the class feel purposeful and professional

## Role structure for 15 students

Use 5 role areas with 3 students in each area.

Role areas:
- Reception
- IT Support
- Administration
- Resources and Printing
- Operations and Records

Each team has:
- 1 lead
- 2 support workers

Roles should rotate:
- weekly, or
- every 2 to 3 sessions if you want more variety

## Role 1: Reception

### Main purpose

Reception is the front desk of the classroom.

### Responsibilities

- welcome students into the room
- remind students to sign into their computer
- check that everyone is at the correct workstation
- alert the trainer if someone cannot log in
- help direct students to the day’s first task
- help keep the room calm and organised at the start of class

### Skills students practise

- greeting people
- verbal communication
- routine checking
- confidence
- professionalism

## Role 2: IT Support

### Main purpose

IT Support helps with simple device and login readiness.

### Responsibilities

- check that computers are on and ready
- help students open the correct form, website, or file
- check chargers, mice, keyboards, and headphones
- report equipment problems to the trainer
- help students find the correct sign-in form bookmark
- support simple troubleshooting before escalating

### Skills students practise

- digital confidence
- troubleshooting
- following steps
- problem reporting
- peer support

## Role 3: Administration

### Main purpose

Administration supports simple office-style paperwork and digital admin.

### Responsibilities

- check that forms are completed properly
- help update simple class trackers if asked
- support file naming and basic digital filing
- prepare labels, lists, or simple typed records
- check whether required entries are missing
- help maintain neat, accurate admin routines

### Skills students practise

- data entry
- accuracy
- file handling
- digital organisation
- attention to detail

## Role 4: Resources and Printing

### Main purpose

Resources and Printing prepares and manages classroom materials.

### Responsibilities

- print handouts if instructed
- collate and staple learning packs
- distribute worksheets and resources
- check paper, pens, notebooks, and supplies
- collect unused materials at the end
- keep the resource table tidy

### Skills students practise

- following instructions
- material handling
- checking quality
- organisation
- practical office support

## Role 5: Operations and Records

### Main purpose

Operations and Records supports end-to-end classroom systems.

### Responsibilities

- check that sign-ins and sign-outs are complete
- help monitor attendance completion
- support the daily checklist
- help reset the room at the end of class
- check that chairs, desks, and devices are left correctly
- report missing sign-outs, damaged equipment, or other issues

### Skills students practise

- accountability
- record checking
- workplace routines
- shared responsibility
- operational awareness

## Team lead responsibilities

The lead in each team should:
- make sure the team understands the role
- divide the work fairly
- report problems to the trainer
- confirm the role tasks are completed
- model calm and respectful behaviour

The lead is not the boss.
The lead is the student responsible for helping the team stay organised.

## Daily classroom workflow

## 1. Start of session

- Reception welcomes and directs students
- Students sign into computers
- IT Support helps with access problems
- Resources and Printing prepares materials
- Administration checks that required setup tasks are done
- Operations and Records checks the room is ready

## 2. During class

- Students complete learning activities
- Role teams complete short support tasks as needed
- Leads report issues to the trainer
- Students practise workplace communication while learning

## 3. End of session

- Students sign out of computers
- Administration and Operations check the records
- Resources team collects and tidies materials
- IT Support checks devices are left correctly
- Reception helps with orderly exit if needed
- Leads confirm that their team tasks are complete

## Non-negotiable expectations

All students must:
- speak respectfully
- stay on task
- follow instructions
- take turns
- look after equipment
- report problems instead of ignoring them
- complete both learning tasks and role duties

## Suggested rotation model

Week 1:
- Group A: Reception
- Group B: IT Support
- Group C: Administration
- Group D: Resources and Printing
- Group E: Operations and Records

Week 2:
- Group A: IT Support
- Group B: Administration
- Group C: Resources and Printing
- Group D: Operations and Records
- Group E: Reception

Continue rotating so every student experiences each role.

## Suggested student grouping

With 15 students:
- divide the class into 5 groups of 3
- keep stronger students mixed with students who need support
- use more confident students as team leads when needed

This helps:
- peer learning
- confidence building
- real teamwork

## Good starter tasks for each role

Reception:
- check everyone has signed in
- greet late arrivals and direct them to the first task

IT Support:
- check bookmarks are open on all computers
- help students find the correct file or website

Administration:
- check the sign-in spreadsheet for missing names
- help rename files correctly

Resources and Printing:
- hand out worksheets
- prepare activity packs

Operations and Records:
- check who has not signed out
- complete the room reset checklist

## Things students should not do

Students should not:
- access confidential student notes
- change official records without instruction
- handle sensitive staff or payroll information
- use teacher logins
- send official emails unless supervised
- make decisions about compliance or formal reporting

## What the trainer does

The trainer remains responsible for:
- final decisions
- supervision
- sensitive records
- conflict management
- formal reporting
- official communication

The student role system is there to support the classroom, not replace teacher oversight.

## Suggested introduction to students

You can explain it like this:

`This classroom will run more like a workplace. Everyone will have a role. That means we will practise being organised, responsible, and helpful, not just completing learning tasks.`

## Simple success measures

You will know the system is working if:
- students sign in and out consistently
- setup time is faster
- fewer small issues come to the trainer
- materials are easier to manage
- students speak more professionally
- students take pride in their responsibilities

## Recommended next step

After introducing the roles, create:
- a role roster
- a daily checklist
- short role cards for each team

These will make the system easier to run every day.
