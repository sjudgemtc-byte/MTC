# Classroom Office Onboarding Process

Prepared: 2026-06-07

Purpose: help new students enter the classroom office model smoothly, understand expectations quickly, and become part of the routine without confusion.

Use with:
- `2026-06-07_MTC_Redfern_RED-01_Pathway_Classroom-Office_Roles-and-Responsibilities.md`
- `2026-06-07_MTC_Redfern_RED-01_Pathway_Classroom-Office_One-Month-Implementation-Plan.md`

## Main goal

Every new student should be able to:
- understand how the classroom works
- follow the sign-in and sign-out routine
- understand that everyone has a role
- join a group without feeling lost
- know what respectful workplace behaviour looks like

## Core onboarding message

Use simple language:

`This classroom runs like a small office. Everyone has responsibilities. We learn, but we also practise being organised, respectful, and ready for work.`

## What every new student needs to learn first

In the first session, the student needs to know:
- where to sit
- how to sign into a computer
- how to sign out at the end
- where to find the day’s task
- how to ask for help
- what their first group role is

Do not overload them with too much information on day one.

## Onboarding process: first day

## 1. Welcome and orientation

The trainer or Reception team should:
- greet the student
- show them where to sit
- explain the sign-in routine
- explain that the room runs like a workplace
- reassure them that they will be supported

Suggested wording:

`You do not need to know everything today. We will show you the system step by step.`

## 2. Computer sign-in induction

Show the student:
- the bookmark or link for the sign-in form
- how to enter their name
- how to choose the computer number
- how to choose `Sign in`
- how to submit the form

Then show them:
- how the same form is used again later for `Sign out`

## 3. Classroom routine induction

Explain the daily pattern:
- arrive
- sign in
- check the daily task
- complete learning tasks
- complete role duties
- pack up
- sign out

Keep this explanation short and repeated over time.

## 4. Introduce the office-style model

Explain:
- everyone belongs to a group
- every group has a responsibility
- responsibilities rotate
- the classroom works better when everyone contributes

Important:
- present roles as workplace practice, not as punishment

## 5. Place the student into a group

For the first few sessions:
- place the new student in a supportive group
- avoid making them a team lead immediately
- pair them with stronger or calmer students
- give them one manageable responsibility first

Good first tasks:
- hand out worksheets
- check bookmarks
- check who has signed in
- help tidy resources

## 6. Explain the non-negotiables

All new students should be told clearly:
- speak respectfully
- follow instructions
- ask for help when stuck
- look after equipment
- complete both learning tasks and role tasks

## 7. End-of-day sign-out induction

Before the student leaves, show them:
- how to open the sign-in form again
- how to choose `Sign out`
- how to complete pack-up correctly

This step matters because sign-out is part of the system, not an optional extra.

## Onboarding process: first week

During the first week, the student should:
- repeat the sign-in and sign-out process daily
- stay in the same group for stability
- learn one role properly before rotating
- be reminded of routines every session

The trainer should:
- check that the student understands the basic system
- notice where they are confused
- keep explanations calm and practical
- praise routine and effort

## Suggested onboarding checklist

Use this checklist for each new student:

- welcomed into the room
- shown where to sit
- shown how to sign in
- shown how to sign out
- shown where to find daily work
- assigned to a group
- given a simple first role
- told the classroom expectations
- checked at the end of the session

## Best first responsibilities for new students

Choose low-pressure responsibilities first:
- Resources and Printing support
- Reception support
- IT Support helper

Avoid first-day tasks such as:
- team lead responsibilities
- checking official records independently
- tasks needing lots of initiative without support

## What the onboarding student should hear often

Useful repeated messages:
- `We do this the same way each day.`
- `Ask early if you are not sure.`
- `You are learning the system as well as the class work.`
- `You do not need to get it perfect straight away.`

## Who supports onboarding

Trainer:
- gives the main explanation
- assigns the student to a group
- checks understanding

Reception:
- helps the student arrive and settle

IT Support:
- helps with device setup and the sign-in form

Group members:
- model what to do
- include the student
- explain simple steps if needed

## Signs onboarding is working

You will know the onboarding process is working if the student:
- signs in with less prompting
- understands where to go and what to do
- joins the group without confusion
- asks for help appropriately
- begins completing small role tasks

## Signs the student needs more support

Provide extra support if the student:
- repeatedly forgets sign-in or sign-out
- does not understand the daily sequence
- looks lost when role tasks begin
- avoids asking for help
- becomes overwhelmed by too much information

If this happens:
- reduce the complexity
- repeat the routine
- keep the student in a stable group longer
- give one clear task at a time

## Recommended onboarding rule

For the first week:
- keep the student in one group
- give one simple support role
- repeat the same routine each session

Only increase responsibility when the student is confident with the basics.
