# Classroom Office Group Responsibility Process

Prepared: 2026-06-07

Purpose: define how student groups share responsibility, complete role tasks, and stay accountable in the classroom office model.

Use with:
- `2026-06-07_MTC_Redfern_RED-01_Pathway_Classroom-Office_Roles-and-Responsibilities.md`
- `2026-06-07_MTC_Redfern_RED-01_Pathway_Classroom-Office_One-Month-Implementation-Plan.md`

## Main goal

Group responsibility means:
- each group has a real function
- each student contributes
- no one carries the whole job alone
- group tasks are completed reliably
- students learn teamwork and accountability

## Basic structure

The class is divided into:
- 5 groups
- 3 students per group

Each group has:
- 1 lead
- 2 support members

Role areas:
- Reception
- IT Support
- Administration
- Resources and Printing
- Operations and Records

## Group responsibility principles

Every group should understand:
- the task belongs to the group, not one student
- everyone helps
- the lead checks progress but does not dominate
- the group must finish the task, not just begin it
- respectful communication is part of the job

## Group process at the start of class

At the start of each session, each group should:

1. check what its role is for the day
2. check what needs to be done first
3. divide the task simply
4. begin the role job straight away
5. tell the trainer if there is a problem

Example:

Reception group:
- one student checks arrivals
- one student reminds classmates about sign-in
- one student directs students to the first task

## Group process during class

During class, each group should:
- complete any role tasks that arise
- help each other if one student is unsure
- stay calm and communicate clearly
- return to learning tasks when the role task is finished

The role should support the classroom, not take over the whole lesson.

## Group process at the end of class

At the end of each session, each group should:

1. complete final role duties
2. check that nothing has been missed
3. tell the trainer if there is a problem
4. confirm that the task is complete

Example:

Operations and Records group:
- check who has not signed out
- check desks and equipment
- report any issues

## Team lead process

The team lead should:
- make sure everyone knows the task
- help divide the work
- check whether the task is actually completed
- report back to the trainer if needed

The lead should not:
- do all the work
- order people around aggressively
- exclude quieter students

## Support member process

Support members should:
- complete the job they are given
- ask questions if unsure
- help the lead keep the group moving
- report if something is not working

Support members are not passive.
They are active contributors to the group responsibility.

## Simple role division model

Each group can divide work as:

- Person 1: starter
- Person 2: support
- Person 3: checker

Example:

Resources and Printing:
- starter gets the materials
- support distributes them
- checker makes sure nothing is missing

This prevents one student doing everything.

## Accountability process

Every group should be accountable for:
- starting the task
- completing the task
- reporting problems
- leaving the work in a good state

Simple accountability questions:
- Did the group do the job?
- Was the job complete?
- Did everyone contribute?
- Was the communication respectful?

## What happens if a group is not functioning well

If a group is struggling:
- simplify the task
- restate the job clearly
- assign smaller sub-tasks
- support the lead
- move a stronger student into the group if needed

Do not respond by making the system more complicated.

## What happens if one student does all the work

If one student is carrying the group:
- stop and reset the task
- divide the work more clearly
- remind the group that responsibility is shared
- ask each student to explain their part

The system works best when contribution is visible.

## What happens if students avoid responsibility

If students avoid role tasks:
- restate the expectation clearly
- connect the task to workplace behaviour
- make the task smaller and more specific
- check whether the role was too vague

Avoid vague instructions such as:
- `Just help out`

Use specific instructions such as:
- `Check the sign-in sheet for missing names`
- `Hand out one worksheet to each computer`

## Group reflection process

At the end of some sessions, ask each group:
- What did your group do well today?
- What was unclear?
- What needs to be easier tomorrow?

Keep this short.
One minute is enough.

## Behaviour expectations inside groups

Students in groups should:
- speak respectfully
- listen to instructions
- take turns
- ask before changing things
- report problems instead of hiding them
- support each other without taking over

## Good signs of strong group responsibility

The group responsibility process is working when:
- students begin tasks without long delays
- quieter students still have a part
- leads can explain what was done
- the classroom runs more smoothly
- the teacher is interrupted less for small issues

## Poor signs to watch for

The process needs adjustment if:
- students wait to be told every tiny step
- one student controls everything
- jobs are started but not finished
- group roles create conflict instead of structure
- students do not see the purpose of the role

## Recommended trainer routine

At the start:
- remind groups of the role

During class:
- check in briefly

At the end:
- ask whether the job was completed

This keeps the system light but consistent.

## Best rule for group responsibility

Use this as the core message:

`The job belongs to the group. Everyone contributes. The lead helps the team stay organised.`
