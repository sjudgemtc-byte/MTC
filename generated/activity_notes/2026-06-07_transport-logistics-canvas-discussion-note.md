# Transport and Logistics Canvas Discussion Note

Prepared: 2026-06-07

## Source page

Canvas weekly discussion starter:

`Before we start this week's course, join the discussion. What do you think transport and logistics means?`

Supporting prompts:
- `Do you get a lot of parcels or deliveries?`
- `Do they come from Australia or overseas?`
- `You can share your ideas and experiences with your classmates and trainer.`

## Purpose of the page

This page works as:
- a discussion starter
- a prior knowledge check
- a vocabulary entry point
- a low-pressure speaking and writing prompt

It is a good lead-in to a transport and logistics topic because it starts with everyday experience rather than technical language.

## Main teaching idea

Students begin with something familiar:
- parcels
- deliveries
- online shopping
- where goods come from

From there, the class can move into:
- transport
- logistics
- supply chains
- fuel costs
- warehousing
- delivery systems

## Suggested trainer model answer

`I think transport and logistics means moving goods from one place to another and making sure they arrive in the right place at the right time. It includes packing, storing, loading, tracking, and delivering items.`

`I do get parcels and deliveries sometimes. Some come from Australia and some come from overseas. When I order something online, I know that many steps happen before it reaches me. I also think transport can be expensive because the price of fuel affects delivery costs and the final price of goods.`

## Why this prompt works

- students can answer from real life
- there is no single perfect answer
- it encourages discussion
- it introduces workplace language naturally
- it can be extended into speaking, writing, reading, or vocabulary work

## Cambridge Dictionary activity idea

This page could lead into a simple dictionary activity using Cambridge Dictionary.

Possible task flow:

1. Ask students to read the discussion prompt.
2. Ask students to highlight or list key words:
   - transport
   - logistics
   - parcel
   - delivery
   - overseas
3. Students look up the words in Cambridge Dictionary.
4. Students write:
   - the word
   - a simple meaning
   - one example sentence
5. Students share one word with the class.

## Possible follow-up questions

- What is the difference between transport and logistics?
- What jobs are part of transport and logistics?
- Why do deliveries from overseas sometimes take longer?
- How does fuel price affect transport?
- What happens to a parcel before it reaches the customer?
